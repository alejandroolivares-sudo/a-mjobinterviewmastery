Behavioral Interview Inquiries

Behavioral interview questions are inquiries that try to predict a candidate’s future on-the-job performance by asking for specific examples of past behavior.[cite: 2] These questions are trying to identify experience, soft skills, and problem-solving capacity, usually starting with a phrase such as "Tell me about a time..." to assess how a candidate handles challenges, conflict, and teamwork.[cite: 2]

The STAR Structural Approach

One of the best approaches to handle this question is the STAR method.[cite: 2] In simple terms is a way to structure your answer so it can paint the picture of the situation, what you were asked to do, your actions, and the result that you got.[cite: 2]

S

Situation

Simply what was the situation that was next to be solved or handled. Be as specific as you considered so the interviewer can get the full picture.[cite: 2]

T

Task

What you were supposed or asked to do.[cite: 2]

A

Actions

Break down step by step what you did, it is ok to show off a little to illustrate your know-how and capacities.[cite: 2]

R

Results

What was the outcome, if you can use metrics to illustrate it even better.[cite: 2]

Pre-Interview Protocol Checklist

  • Read about the company: Focus directly on Goals, Upcoming or present projects, Policy, and Things that capture your attention.[cite: 2]
  • Use the mirror technique: Try to imitate your interviewer. If he or she is very active and uses a lot of body language, do the same. If they are very serious, be serious yourself too.[cite: 2]
  • Build Initial Connection: Use questions as “tell me about yourself” or “how did you find out about this job” to create rapport and build a connection with your interviewer.[cite: 2]
  • Curate Personal Stories: Think of stories that emphasize your skills but don’t make them too long. Keep track of weaknesses, strengths, and difficult situations you have overcome.[cite: 2]
  • Connect Past to Future: Connect how your previous experiences might help to affront this new job.[cite: 2]
  • Salary Assessment: Research the approximate salary and benefits companies are offering for the job you want to apply for.[cite: 2]
